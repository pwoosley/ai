Course Description & Structure

This course promotes understanding of computer programming and logic by teaching students to “think like a computer.” It covers variables, control/data structures, and principles of command-line and object-oriented languages.

Format: Lecture and Labs

Required Materials

Textbook: Programming Logic and Design, 10th Edition by Joyce Farrell

Access: The textbook is available electronically in the Blackboard Course Shell. Students are charged a fee on their tuition bill for program access.

Workplace Ethics & Attendance

For this course, no more than 3 days may be missed.
  • If a student is absent for more than 15% of scheduled class sessions, the student will be withdrawn.
  • Faculty do not differentiate between “excused” and “un-excused” absences.
  • Before mid-point: Student is withdrawn and a "W" is issued.
  • After mid-point: Student is issued a failing grade "E".
